Processors

The proccessor also reffered to as the CPU is one of the most important parts of a computer. It acts as the brains of the computer and tells all the parts how to communitcate with eachother. It is crucial to get a CPU based on your needs to make sure you are satisfied with the performance of your PC.

Entry Level

A great value CPU that performs very well in both everyday tasks and high load programs.

AMD Ryzen 5 3600
A 6 core 12 thread processor with great performance for gaming, streaming and multitasking. Desinged for AM4 socket motherboards and also PCIe 4.0 compatible. The Ryzen 5 3600 is a fully unlocked CPU which allows you to overclock giving you the maximum performance the CPU can get. The 3600 comes with a 3.6 GHz base clock and a boost clock of 4.2 GHz. The 3600 is compatible with PCIe 4.0 which lets you get super fast speeds when it comes to NVMe SSDs.
Mid End

A great CPU for people who want to play AAA games at high framerates or require good performance for high load tasks.

AMD Ryzen 5 5600x
The Ryzen 5 5600x comes with 6 cores and 12 threads. The 5600x has a 3.7GHz base clock with a boost clock all the way up to 4.6GHz. This CPU is great for gaming, streaming and other 3D applications. A great choice for people who want to play AAA games without worrying about bottleneck or others who want fast rendering for productivity uses.
High End

The best CPU you can get your hands on for those who don't need to worry about budget.

AMD Ryzen 9 5950x
The 5950x comes with a whopping 16 cores and 32 threads and has a base clock of 3.4 GHz and a boost clock of 4.9GHz. The 5950x has the best single and multicore performance yet. This powerhouse can obliterate any game or 3D rendering thrown at it without a problem. Even better, the 5950x can be pushed even further as it is an unlocked processor letting you push out the maximum performance.
